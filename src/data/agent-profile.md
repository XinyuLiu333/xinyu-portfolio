## AI-Human Relationship Exploration (2026)

A philosophical thinking piece — not a shipped product but a record of thinking. Five documents, zero screens. Developed before any implementation to define the interaction philosophy for an enterprise AI platform.

**The "Air" metaphor**: The ideal AI experience is like air — invisible, essential, omnipresent, variable but never absent. Most AI products are destinations you visit; the best AI dissolves into where you already work.

**Seven design principles**:
1. Invisible medium, not fixed interface
2. Observe first, propose second, never impose
3. Three doors to one room (describe, remix, or teach — all produce the same governed object)
4. Everything is a draft until you say otherwise
5. Honest about uncertainty
6. You can always start over
7. Multiplayer is opt-in, scoped, and reversible

**Three-layer experience model**:
- Contextual Cards: lightweight intelligence surfaced in Slack, Outlook, calendar, mobile — most users never open an app
- Intent Surface: structured entry organized by outcome (Prepare, Communicate, Synthesize, Automate, Monitor)
- My Tools: personal collection of tools that work, with run history, health indicators, and sharing controls

**Anti-patterns rejected**: "Configure before you use", "Open the app to get started", "Pick a mode", "Here is your workflow builder", "Done automatically for you", "Chat with the AI" as the home screen.

## Design Philosophy (Recurring Patterns Across All Work)

**Systems thinking**: Journey maps, service blueprints, end-to-end lifecycle design. Maps the full workflow before touching any screen. Designed the Client Activation journey, Event journey, migraine attack lifecycle, inhaler onboarding service blueprint, IRB submission-review cycle, and proposal lifecycle.

**Research-grounded**: Ethnographic research, 1:1 interviews, participatory design, Jobs-to-be-Done. 91 stakeholders for IRB. 128 colleagues + 20 Partners for CRM. Multiple rounds of field testing for inhalers. Behavior design frameworks for migraine.

**Behavior design**: Nudges, progressive disclosure, cognitive load reduction, trust calibration. Designs for real human behavior, not ideal behavior. Understands that consulting Partners keep track of college basketball teams because clients are fans, host dinners at home, and text clients to reduce friction.

**Push over pull**: Value delivered before users ask. Morning briefing emails before anyone opens an app. Pre-meeting intelligence pushed to mobile. Contextual cards in existing tools. The system goes to the user, not the other way around.

**AI interaction model**: Observe first, propose second, never impose. Everything starts as a draft. Trust is earned and delegated on a spectrum. The system is honest about uncertainty. Calibrated honesty over polished plausibility. One clarifying question beats a plausible-looking wrong answer that reaches a client.

**Enterprise adoption**: Deep sensitivity to organizational culture. Understands that McKinsey Partners resist tools that feel like surveillance or scorekeeping. Pipeline tracking clashes with the firm's anti-sales ethos. 99% of energy on iteration, change management, and role modeling — not just launching features.

**Prototyping as validation**: Builds high-fidelity prototypes to validate before engineering investment. The AI Proposal Copilot was vibe-coded as a working React app. The Activate platform was built as a working prototype with dashboard, nudge engine, Contact 360 dossiers, conversational AI, and cadence-driven outreach.
