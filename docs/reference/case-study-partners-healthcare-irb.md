# Partners Healthcare IRB — Case Study Content

**Source:** xinyuliu.com/web-app-design + goinvo.com/work/partners-insight/
**Company:** GoInvo for Partners Healthcare
**Year:** 2015
**Role:** Designer (joined mid-process, worked with Jennifer Patel at Involution Studios)
**Duration:** 20 months total (2-5 designers and engineers)

## Overview

Redesign of Insight, the electronic IRB (Institutional Review Board) portal used by Partners Healthcare — the largest provider network in Massachusetts. All Partners-affiliated investigators must submit studies through the Partners Human Research Committee (PHRC). Insight is the central point of ingest and administration for medical research for six major research institutions. The software and processes suffered from serious usability issues that delayed research by weeks.

## Problem

### For Submitters (Research Teams):
- Difficulty parsing complex language in lengthy applications — felt like "decoding a puzzle"
- Couldn't easily find their protocols — saw all department protocols, not their own, had to search by system ID number
- Protocol information fragmented across multiple layers and locations
- No visibility into where their protocol was held up or why
- Reviewer comments were enumerated in a separate text area, referencing areas by number — submitters had to decipher and hunt for problem areas

### For Reviewers (IRB Committee):
- Spent significant time hunting for specific pieces within protocols
- No side-by-side comparison for protocol versions — had to open previous version in a new window and compare line by line
- Managed dozens of studies daily, working with frequently frustrated research teams
- Tracked recommendations and instructions across weeks-long processes

### For Administrators:
- High turnover (many were Fellows) meant new people needed to get acquainted quickly
- Staff supported multiple protocols from different teams, needing to collect all materials — time-consuming and frustrating

## Process

### In-depth, In-person Interviews
Conducted preliminary round with **65 individuals at 6 research facilities**, each with a hand in the protocol review process. Investigated the end-to-end IRB process to develop appreciation for and understanding of the people, how they thought about their work, and the tools they used.

### Stakeholder Groups Studied:
1. **IRB Reviewers** — individuals with clinical or administrative backgrounds, inspecting protocols for errors or unethical research. Expedited reviewers spent at least 5 days/week, 8+ hours/day within Insight.
2. **Research Team Members** — not always clinicians or scientists. Staff supporting administration or assisting PIs. Worked with multiple protocols from different departments. Often Fellows with high turnover.
3. **Principal Investigators** — leading the research studies.
4. **Program Chairs and Admins** — overseeing the review process.

### Key Pain Points Identified:
- Confusing language throughout the application
- Poor protocol findability (search by ID number only)
- Fragmented protocol information
- Opaque review status
- Unclear reviewer comments (enumerated lists referencing scattered form locations)
- No version comparison capability

## Solution

### 1. Protocol Findability: "Where is my protocol?!"
- Grouped protocols by study (not flat list of all department protocols)
- Sorted by most recently updated
- Color-coded status to highlight protocols needing attention
- Improved response time from both submission and review teams
- Older/inactive protocols fall to bottom

### 2. Open and Navigable Forms
- Concise, clear language replacing puzzle-like legal text
- Typographic best practices for scanning and readability
- Contextual help throughout
- Application navigable to different parts at any point in submission or review
- Progress tracking with visible form completion status

### 3. Transparent Review Process
- Clear step and status visibility — from protocol list to detail view
- Play-by-play history of submission and review (who had it and when)
- Eliminated stress from not knowing where protocol was held up

### 4. Contextual Communication
- Reviewers could make comments directly where changes were needed (not in a separate letter)
- Comments marked in protocol navigation
- Clear back-and-forth communication between submission and review
- Reduced mental stress and time for submitters

### 5. Clear Historical Views
- Versioning with access to previous instantiations
- Side-by-side comparison capability
- Changes and comments marked clearly in protocol navigation

### 6. Foundational Design System
- Inventory of all screens
- Cataloged base components and reusable styles
- Consulted on front-end engineering, UAT, and QA

## Participatory Design

Returned to same groups of reviewers and submitters, occasionally pulling in fresh voices. Spoke with **91 individuals overall**. Collected feedback and worked through tricky situations with their input. During final presentations, participants felt heard and had a responsible part in the design.

## Impact

- Validated with 91 individuals (analysts, admins, program chairs, principal investigators)
- Stakeholders approved designs and anticipated the release
- **Insight 4.0 rolled out to all Partners research facilities**
- Design system established as foundation for the larger Insight application
