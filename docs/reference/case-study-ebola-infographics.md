## Overview

Two related visual resources created during the 2014 Ebola outbreak at GoInvo (Involution Studios) in Arlington, MA. The team developed educational materials to address the information crisis during the outbreak and support healthcare workers.

## Problem

During the 2014 Ebola outbreak, confusion and conflicting information from news outlets created widespread public anxiety and misinformation. Healthcare workers needed clear, visual guidance on Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) protocols — where improper use could lead to heat-related illness or infection.

The West Africa outbreak (Guinea, Liberia, Sierra Leone, 2013-2016) resulted in over 28,000 cases and 11,000 deaths. The weighted case fatality rate was approximately 65%.

## Solution

### 1. Understanding Ebola: A Visual Guide

Comprehensive visual guide released in October 2014 to educate the public about the epidemic. Covered:
- Ebola transmission (not airborne; requires direct contact with bodily fluids)
- Symptoms and progression (flu-like illness → progressive weakness → multi-organ failure)
- Epidemiology (4 species pathogenic to humans; 2-21 day incubation period)
- Prevention and containment

### 2. Ebola Care Guideline

Illustrated process guide focused on Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) used by healthcare workers in Ebola treatment areas.

Key insight addressed: The recommended PPE — waterproof apron, surgical gown, surgical cap, respirator, face shield, boots, and two layers of gloves — significantly reduces the body's normal heat dissipation through sweating. The excess heat and moisture trapped inside, combined with physical effort while carrying extra weight, increases healthcare workers' risk for heat-related illnesses.

The guideline provided clear, illustrated step-by-step PPE donning and doffing procedures to minimize both infection risk and heat-related illness.

## Impact

- Published resources used for public education during the outbreak
- PDF guideline distributed to healthcare organizations
- Featured on RISD's "Our RISD" showcase
