## Overview

An interactive website and ebook design for the article "From Bathroom to Healthroom," which creates a new experience of storytelling and improves user engagement. Integrates UX design, infographics design, motion design, videography, and digital book design. Worked with engineers to manage prototypes and improve functionality.

## Problem

Health, as an experience and idea, is undergoing an epic shift. For millennia, humans have treated health as a rare spike requiring intervention. We are most conscious of our health during episodic events — a visit to the doctor, an ER trip. For the average person without chronic illness, health is conceived of and managed as an exception.

Yet health is the single most important factor for any human. The potential impact of a health setback reaches into all areas of life — work, finances, love, hobbies — and affects the community. The complexity of health has surged dramatically over the past 100 years.

The article explores this transition from episodic medical care to continuous health monitoring, but needed a compelling, immersive digital format to engage readers.

## Content & Solution

The interactive website tells the story through:

### Health History Timeline
- 10,000 BC: Transition to agriculture
- 4000 BC: Formation of human groups
- 3000 BC: Earliest surgical sutures in ancient Egypt
- 400 BC: Hippocrates on water quality
- 1920: Modern medicine begins
- 2018: EHR data on subdermal chip
- 2019: Bathroom becomes Healthroom

### The Sensor Revolution
Current wearable health devices (Basis watch, Fitbit pedometer, BodyMedia band, Jawbone Up, Philips pedometer) demonstrate the direction — but suffer from the "non-forgettables" problem: users forget to wear them, charge them, switch modes. Form factor isn't really The Question.

Hardware is the gateway drug to services and data. Current devices are about information and decision-making, not hardware commodity.

### The Healthroom Vision
Future: as you walk around your house, stand at your desk, go to the bathroom — physiological data is captured invisibly to do DSP analysis signaling "getting worse" or "getting better." Machine learning, big data, and design crash together.

### hGraph
Open source, standardized visual representation of a patient's health status. Users can easily identify which metrics are in normal range vs. too high or low. Effectively conveys data at both large and small sizes.
